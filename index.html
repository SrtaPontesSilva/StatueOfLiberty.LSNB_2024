Statue of Liberty

Location

The statue is located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor.

Construction

Construction of the Statue of Liberty was completed in 1884 in France and shipped to the United States, where it was assembled and dedicated on October 28, 1886.

Artist & Engineers

The statue was designed by French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi. Gustave Eiffel, known for designing the Eiffel Tower, was responsible for the internal structure. Engineer Eugène Viollet-le-Duc initially collaborated on the project.

Inspiration

The statue was inspired by the ideas of freedom and democracy, representing the Roman goddess Libertas.

Purpose

The Statue of Liberty was built as a symbol of freedom, representing the friendship between France and the United States and celebrating the ideals of freedom and democracy.

Did You Know?

The Crown

The crown has seven points, symbolizing the seven seas and continents.

The Torch

The torch represents the light that guides the way to freedom.

The Pedestal

The pedestal contains an inscription of the poem "The New Colossus" by Emma Lazarus, which speaks of welcoming immigrants.

Broken Chains

The broken chains at the statue's feet symbolize the liberation from oppression.

The New Colossus

by Emma Lazarus

"Not like the brazen giant of Greek fame,
With conquering limbs astride from land to land;
Here at our sea-washed, sunset gates shall stand
A mighty woman with a torch, whose flame
Is the imprisoned lightning, and her name
Mother of Exiles. From her beacon-hand
Glows world-wide welcome; her mild eyes command
The air-bridged harbor that twin cities frame.
“Keep, ancient lands, your storied pomp!” cries she
With silent lips. “Give me your tired, your poor,
Your huddled masses yearning to breathe free,
The wretched refuse of your teeming shore.
Send these, the homeless, tempest-tost to me,
I lift my lamp beside the golden door!”
